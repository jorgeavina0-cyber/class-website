Group video project

During week 14, you will work with your group to make a video explaining an differential geometry topic of your choice (probably related to at least one member's blog post). During week 15 we will watch these videos and host a "Question and Answer" session after each video.

Goals

  • Teach your classmates about some interesting ideas in Differential Geometry!
  • Get more comfortable using "imperfect takes."
  • Learn how to make and upload a YouTube video.
  • Learn the basics of video editing software.

Guidelines

  1. You have license to make any style of video that you would like, ranging from a Numberphile-style video to a series of short-format TikTok videos.
  2. Your video (or series of videos) should be 10 minutes in length, with a strict requirement to be 8–15 minutes total.
  3. Your video should contain every member of your group appearing either in the video or as a video voice-over.
  4. Follow each of the steps listed in the checklists below.

Checklists

Rules like these are allowed to be broken, but make sure you have a good reason to break them!

Getting started checklist

  • Watch some examples both from the Video Inspiration section below and student videos posted on Discord.
  • Watch at least 2.5 minutes of the Grant Sanderson advice video embedded below, starting at 8:32.
  • Devise a rough outline for a 8–15 minute video aimed at your classmates.

Pre-recording checklist

  • Find a time for your group to get together to film together. (No filming on your own!)
  • Gather two or more cameras with microphones (screen capture can count here).
  • Plan to film actual handwriting on a surface that is not a chalkboard or whiteboard. (No videos that are just slide shows.)

Editing checklist

  • Include at least two "camera angles."
  • Edit audio to ensure that volume is consistent.
  • Include an image or external video with audio. (An image from the blog post could be a good choice here.)
  • Ensure that the video is between 8–15 minutes.

Wrapping up checklist

  • Upload the video to a video service such as YouTube (can be unlisted).
  • Ensure that the video title is interesting, catchy, and descriptive.
  • Include a meaningful video description.
  • Spend a few minutes trying to anticipate some questions you might get about the video when you play it in class on Monday, May 4th.
  • Turn it in on Gradescope by providing a PDF that simply contains a link to the video! Group submissions are turned on. If you use the \usepackage{hyperref} at the top of your LaTeX file, then you can include the URL with \url{...}.
