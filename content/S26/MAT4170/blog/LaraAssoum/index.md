## 1.1 Introduction 

* Genetic code and its structure
  * The system of where living cells translate information into protein 
  * Consists of 4 nucleotides : 
  * DNA : A, T, C, G
  * RNA : A, U, C, G
  * Also consists of nucleotides called codons (3 nucleotides is 1 codon) 
  * There are 64 possible codons 
  * Codons are meant to start / stop signals 
  * If there are multiple codons, then it is the same amino acid 

* Permutation Groups and their structure 
  * A permutation is the rearrangement of elements in a set 
  * A permutation group is made up of the following : 
  * {Identity, Closure, Inverse, & Associativity} 
  * An example could be that the set {1, 2, 3} is written as (permutation) (123), (132), etc. 
  * There’s also the symmetric group (Sₙ), which tells us how the amount of elements within a permutation 
  * We can use permutation groups to determine the transformation and symmetry of something

## 1.3 Contrasting Genetic Code vs. Permutation Groups 
###	1.3.1. Genetic Code
The main purpose of the genetic code is to produce proteins for us, something we all need. Although it is essential, the genetic system is not structured to where it is “perfect”. That is where they differ from permutation groups. Mutations do happen randomly which makes it hard for us to predict the genetic code. While some genetic codes may follow patterns, the rest could be affected by several factors. 
Genetics are also something that is not stable. They are always evolving and changing, especially over time. You can inherit new traits over time because mutations are capable of altering genetic sequences. With that being said, a mathematical system is not considered to be as flexible as the genetic code. 

## 1.3.2 Permutation Groups 
Permutation groups are completely derived and made up from mathematical concepts and ideas. There is a format that they have to hold and rules that they obey. Unlike genetic codes, mathematical concepts, in this case permutation groups, do not change over time. They are considered to be stable, but what could change is perhaps the way a mathematical concept is explained. 
Unlike genetics, permutation groups are also predictable. Each operation is followed with the same logic and it is also very consistent. It is never random, you expect what will happen and what the result may look like. 
Permutation groups are mainly used to focus on symmetry and also structure. They can describe the rearrangements of objects which makes them very useful in both science and math. With that being said, in comparison to the genetic code, permutation groups are much more controlled and structured.
